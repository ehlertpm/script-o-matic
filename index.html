Overview

Intro

Hi. This iPad is a learning device for people like you who may be interested in participating in clinical research studies. Over the next several minutes, this video will explain the details of a study you’re being invited to take part in, and help you make a decision about whether you’d like to participate in the study. You’ll learn about your rights and responsibilities, the required study procedures, and the possible risks and benefits of your participation. After watching this video, you’ll take a short knowledge review that will help your doctor assess your understanding of the study and its requirements.

Randomization

This study is a randomized trial, meaning that you will be assigned by chance to one of treatment groups. In other words, you will have a chance of being assigned to any of the treatment groups. . A placebo looks exactly like the study drug but has no active medicine in it. Neither you nor the study doctor will know which treatment group you are in.

Voluntary Participation

You may decide to stop your participation at any time. If you decide to stop your participation, your relationship with your doctor and any treatment that you receive outside of the study will not be affected.

Informed Consent

ICF

In order to enter this study, you have to sign a document called the “informed consent form”. By signing the informed consent form, you are saying that you understand the risks and benefits of this study and that you’d like to volunteer to take part in this study. This iPad will allow you to read the entire informed consent document and to sign it. A copy of the informed consent form, with your signature and the signature of the study doctor or staff member, will be given to you at the end of the process. As you go through the process, you may always ask the study doctor, or one of the study staff, any questions you might have.

Risks & Benefits

Medications

You must discuss taking any medications, including over the counter drugs, herbal medications or supplements, with your doctor before and during your participation in this study. Some medications may interact with the study drug. Your study doctor will tell you what medications you may or may not take while you are participating in this study.

Side Effects

This study drug may cause you to experience side effects such as _____. The informed consent document you’ll review after this video contains a detailed list of side effects that have been reported when using this study drug. Please take a close look at this list so that you can fully understand the possible risks of participating in this study, and ask the study staff or doctor if you have any questions.

Pregnancy

If you are a woman who is currently pregnant or breastfeeding, you are not eligible to participate in this study. If you are a woman of childbearing potential, you must agree not to become pregnant during your participation in this study and for _____ after you receive the study drug. You must agree to use _____ reliable form(s) of birth control approved by your study doctor. If you become pregnant during the study, you’ll be instructed to stop taking the study drug and inform your doctor immediately. If you are a man, you must agree not get a woman pregnant during your participation in the study and for _____ after you receive the study drug. You must agree to use _____ reliable form(s) of birth control approved by your study doctor. If you father a child while taking part in this study, you must inform your doctor immediately.

Benefit Statement

If you decide to take part in this study, your signs and symptoms may or may not improve. Your participation in this study may provide important information to help doctors improve treatment for patients and help others with the same illness as you.

Wrapping Up

Disclosure

The information collected about you during this study may be given to the professionals who are conducting this study. Your information may be used to help develop medical treatments for individuals, like yourself, and for general health research. The professionals who may collect your information include members of the study sponsor, study research staff, companies working for the study sponsor and regulatory authorities. Your identity and medical information will be kept confidential.
